Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is octal format?

Octal is a base-8 numeral system that uses digits from 0 to 7. It is commonly used in computing for representing binary numbers in a compact form.

Why convert text to octal?

Converting text to octal helps visualize or store data in base-8 encoding, often used in programming, file permissions, and Unix systems.
