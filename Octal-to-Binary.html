Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is an octal number system?

The octal number system is a base-8 numeral system that uses digits from 0 to 7. It's often used in computing as a compact representation of binary numbers.

Why convert octal to binary?

Binary is the foundational language of computers. Converting octal to binary helps in interpreting data at the machine level, especially in low-level programming or hardware design.
